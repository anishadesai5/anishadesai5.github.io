Background

At the time of this experiment, Udacity courses currently have two options on the course overview page: "start free trial", and "access course materials".

  • If the student clicks “start free trial”, they will be asked to enter their credit card information, and then they will be enrolled in a free trial for the paid version of the course. After 14 days, they will automatically be charged unless they cancel first.
  • If the student clicks “access course materials”, they will be able to view the videos and take the quizzes for free, but they will not receive coaching support or a verified certificate, and they will not submit their final project for feedback.

    • Experiment Description

    In the experiment, Udacity tested a change where if the student clicked “start free trial”, they were asked how much time they had available to devote to the course.

  • If the student indicated 5 or more hours per week, they would be taken through the checkout process as usual.
  • If they indicated fewer than 5 hours per week, a message would appear indicating that Udacity courses usually require a greater time commitment for successful completion, and suggesting that the student might like to access the course materials for free. At this point, the student would have the option to continue enrolling in the free trial, or access the course materials for free instead.

    Introduction

                     The nature of our work is changing. Due to the rise in remote work in the post pandemic world, the mental health of employees is being compromised. From working at irregular timings to increased workload due to layoffs, the anxiety and depression levels are at a record high. The impact of mental health problems in the workplace has serious consequences not only for the individual but also for the productivity of the organization. It is imperative for companies to come up with unique solutions to tackle these issues.

    Therefore, to assist companies in taking care of their employees, we came up with Espoir meaning ‘Hope for the future’.

    About Espoir

    Espoir App Video

                      Espoir is your user-friendly application which employees can visit to de-stress. They can also look for options which can help them relax from their busy schedules. Espoir main’s focus is to monitor depression and anxiety levels. From providing a chatbot feature to even booking appointments with therapists, Espoir is here to assist you with all that you need. Espoir is built on three core principles:

    1) Privacy is our priority

                      The surveys provided by employees and interactions with the app will be kept private. Eg: If any employee is severely depressed, his/her manager will not know which employee is diagnosed with depression. The manager will only get to know the statistics of his entire team and no personal information of any employee will be provided. Interactions with our chatbot 'Eva' will also be kept private.

    2) Help is at your fingertips

                      Along with our chatbot feature ‘Eva’, employees can go visit our self help resources. The interactive survey can help employees book local hiking trips to drawing classes and much more. They can even book appointments with therapists nearby. Espoir wishes to be the one stop destination for employees to help de-stress them from mental health issues.

    3) Real Time Data for Employers

                      The top management can view the statistics for each department. For eg: the demographics of employees, percentage of employees having depression or anxiety in each department, depression or anxiety levels as per different designations and even a trend line comparing mental health issues over the years. We have incorporated a live Tableau dashboard for the management to view the complete statistics
